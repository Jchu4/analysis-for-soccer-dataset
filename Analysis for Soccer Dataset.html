Introduction¶

Being an avid of soccer fan/player ever since the age of 10, investigating the following European Soccer Database was intriguing for me. It consists of seven tables, namely, 'Country', 'Leauge', 'Player', 'Player Attributes', 'Team', 'Team Attributes', and 'Match'. Briefly, the dataset contains useful statistics across 11 different European Leagues between 2008 and 2016. The orignal source of the database I used can found on Kaggle. You would have to wrangle the data before exploring.

After observing the tables along with deep-seated questions since young, I sought to answer the questions seen below:

Questions :¶

  • Which players have the most penalties?
  • Which players took the most accurate free-kicks?
  • Which team had the most victories at home in the 2015/2016 season?
  • What team attributes led to the most victories?

# combine Match table with combined Team & Team_Attributes table (selecting relevant columns from above)
# using home_team as the linked key 
q7 = """
WITH 
team_df AS
            (
             SELECT 
                 t.team_api_id team_id,
                 t.team_long_name home_team_name,
                 ta.*
             FROM team t
                 INNER JOIN team_attributes ta 
                 ON ta.team_api_id = t.team_api_id
            ),
match_df AS
            (
             SELECT
                 league_id,
                 season, 
                 date date_m, 
                 match_api_id, 
                 home_team_api_id, 
                 away_team_api_id, 
                 home_team_goal, 
                 away_team_goal 
             FROM match 
            )     

SELECT
    m.*,
    t.*
FROM match_df m
INNER JOIN team_df t 
ON t.team_api_id = m.home_team_api_id   
GROUP BY m.season, m.home_team_api_id, t.home_team_name, m.date_m, t.date
"""

Conclusions¶

With all that mentioned, there are certainly limitations to the data set, with a handful teams not having full records, and most teams missing data here and there for different years that would make a difference in gathering the top 10 teams. I am not a 100% sure on whether the way I have pulled the data with my SQL queries were what was best as well (eg. could have been unneccesary dupilicates etc.)

I believe I could have expressed the visualisations in a clearer manner with different kind of plots which I have yet to learn, and draw correlations more easily & quickly.
